Soft Robotic Gripper

Development of a flexible pneumatic gripper for safe interaction with sensitive components.

Overview

This project focuses on developing a soft robotic gripper based on PneuNet structures. These grippers use pneumatically actuated networks that deform in response to air pressure, enabling them to gently grasp delicate objects. Designed and fabricated entirely in-house using rubber molding techniques, this gripper is intended to be mounted on a 3-DOF manipulator for handling sensitive and irregularly shaped parts.

Design & Fabrication

  • Gripper structure cast in silicone elastomer using custom-designed molds
  • Internal chambers designed to expand under pressure, enabling curling motion
  • Mesh reinforcement used for structural stability and directional actuation
  • Multi-layer molding and integration of pneumatic tubes for air supply

Why Soft Robotics?

Soft robotics enables safe and adaptive manipulation, especially for tasks involving humans or fragile objects. Unlike rigid arms, soft grippers conform to the shape of the object, reducing the risk of damage and allowing high compliance.
