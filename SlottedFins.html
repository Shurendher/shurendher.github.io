Thermal Optimization of Fins

Enhancing heat dissipation with reduced material mass through geometric design refinement and simulation.

Overview

In modern thermal systems, fins are used to increase the heat dissipation area of a surface. However, the material and space constraints often demand designs that achieve better performance with minimal mass. This project investigates and compares different fin geometries — primarily rectangular and slotted configurations — to find the optimal design balancing heat transfer and material usage.

Objective

To evaluate and compare the performance of various fin geometries based on their temperature profiles, heat flux, and mass. The goal was to identify which fin design offer superior thermal performance with lowest material consumption using numerical and couputer-aided analysis.

Methodology

  • Modeled five solid geometries: one circular baseline and four variations (square, triangular, trapezoidal, elliptical) and selected the highest heat transfer geometry. The perimeter of all the profiles are same.
  • Proposed optimized model based on the profile with highest heat transfer rate (With slot and rectangular grooves).
  • Modeled the geometries using CATIA V5 and imported into ANSYS Workbench for meshing and steady-state thermal simulation.
  • Conducted analysis under constant heat input and convective boundary conditions.
  • Compared results based on heat transfer rate and material consumption for Aluminium 6082. The cylinder bore temperature, convection co-efficient and the ambient temperature are assumed to be 350 °C, 12 W/m²°C and 30 °C respectively.

Key Results

  • The heat transfer in triangular profile exhibited the highest (82552 W/m²) and circular profile (71441 W/m²) showed the lowest.
  • The slotted and grooved design, created based on the triangular profile was analyzed.
  • Due to significant increas in surface area for convection, the profile with slots exhibited 93.3% (221750 W/m²) increase in heat transfer.
  • Similarly, grooved profile had 49.8% (200050 W/m²) increase in heat transfer.
  • Comparing the mass of cylinder for different fin profiles, slotted (2.30 kg) had the lowest followed by grooved (2.33 kg) and triangular (2.41 kg). Circular had the highest (2.53 kg).
  • Although slotted fins clearly outperformed other profiles, the ease and cost of manufacturing would be substantially high due to the complexity.
  • Hence, this study provides a direcion for exploration of optimizing fin structure and this could be followed up by a study on manufacturing (through traditional (milling) and modern techniques (SLS printing)) complexity.
