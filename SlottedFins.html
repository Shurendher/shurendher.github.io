Thermal Optimization of Fins

Enhancing heat dissipation with reduced material mass through geometric design refinement and simulation.

Overview

In modern thermal systems, fins are used to increase the heat dissipation area of a surface. However, the material and space constraints often demand designs that achieve better performance with minimal mass. This project investigates and compares different fin geometries â€” primarily rectangular and slotted configurations â€” to find the optimal design balancing heat transfer and material usage.

Objective

To evaluate and compare the performance of various fin geometries based on their temperature profiles, heat flux, and mass. The goal was to identify which fin design offer superior thermal performance with lowest material consumption using numerical and couputer-aided analysis.

Methodology

  • Modeled five geometries: one rectangular baseline and four slotted variations (circular, elliptical, square, hexagonal).
  • All designs were created using SolidWorks with consistent dimensions and boundary conditions.
  • Imported into ANSYS Workbench for meshing and steady-state thermal simulation.
  • Conducted analysis under constant heat input (1 W) and convective boundary conditions.
  • Compared results based on maximum temperature, heat flux, and mass.
