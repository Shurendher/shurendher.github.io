Thermal Optimization of Fins

Enhancing heat dissipation with reduced material mass through geometric design refinement and simulation.

Overview

In modern thermal systems, fins are used to increase the heat dissipation area of a surface. However, the material and space constraints often demand designs that achieve better performance with minimal mass. This project investigates and compares different fin geometries â€” primarily rectangular and slotted configurations â€” to find the optimal design balancing heat transfer and material usage.

Objective

To evaluate and compare the performance of various fin geometries based on their temperature profiles, heat flux, and mass. The goal was to identify which fin design offer superior thermal performance with lowest material consumption using numerical and couputer-aided analysis.

Methodology and Results

  • Modeled five solid geometries: one circular baseline and four variations (square, triangular, trapezoidal, elliptical) and selected the highest heat transfer geometry. The perimeter of all the profiles are same.
  • Proposed optimized model based on the profile with highest heat transfer rate (With slot and rectangular grooves).
  • Modeled the geometries using CATIA V5 and imported into ANSYS Workbench for meshing and steady-state thermal simulation.
  • Conducted analysis under constant heat input and convective boundary conditions.
  • Compared results based on heat transfer rate and material consumption.

  • All slotted designs performed better thermally due to increased surface area and airflow.
  • Circular slotted design identified as most optimal due to its performance and ease of manufacture.
