SMART: Satellite Motion-mapping Robotic Testbed

A tabletop testbed to emulate spacecraft attitude dynamics using 4 reaction wheels.

Overview

SMART (Satellite Motion-mapping Robotic Testbed) is developed in IASRL to simulate satellite attitude control on Earth. The system uses four reaction wheels arranged in a pyramid configuration to enable three-axis attitude control, mimicking real spacecraft behavior through controlled angular momentum exchange.

My Contribution

  • Supported mechanical development of the wheel & subsystem assembly and hardware-softawre interfacing
  • Developed digital twin and validated the performance of testbed in SolidWorks
  • Assisted in debugging control firmware and tuning the IMU-based attitude estimation system
  • Worked closely with the team to balance design trade-offs for structural integrity and performance

Key Features

  • Pyramidal reaction wheel configuration for full 3-DOF torque authority
  • Attitude stabilization using onboard IMU and closed-loop control
  • Low-cost, modular hardware platform for educational and research applications
