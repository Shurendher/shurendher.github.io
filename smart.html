Thermal Optimization of Fins

Enhancing heat transfer with reduced material through design iterations.

Overview

This project investigates the trade-off between heat transfer performance and material usage in fin-like structures. Inspired by existing geometries from literature, the designs were recreated, simulated, and evaluated using FEA tools.

Design and Simulation Process

The workflow began with extracting reference geometries from research papers. These were modeled in CAD, meshed in ANSYS, and simulated for heat transfer. Mass and temperature gradients were recorded to identify optimal designs.

Outcome

Several fin configurations showed increased heat dissipation with a simultaneous reduction in material. The best design achieved 20% improved transfer rate with 15% less mass compared to the baseline geometry.
