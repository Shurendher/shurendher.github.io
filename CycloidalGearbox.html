Design of Cycloidal Gearbox for Nema 17

A compact and robust reduction gearbox design suitable for robotic joints and modular actuation systems.

Overview

Cycloidal gearboxes are ideal for applications that demand high torque, low backlash, and good shock load resistance. This project aims to design a modular cycloidal gearbox with a 9:1 reduction ratio, optimized for use with NEMA 17 stepper motors. The design uses a precision-generated cycloidal profile with 9 lobes and 10 pins, ensuring smooth motion and torque amplification. Components were designed to be 3D printable and easy to assemble, focusing on early prototyping and iterative development for future deployment in robotic arms.

Design Features

  • 9:1 single-stage reduction using 10 pins and 9-lobe cycloidal disc
  • Input via eccentric shaft connected to NEMA 17 motor
  • Compact casing with top and bottom covers and fasteners
  • Designed with tolerances suitable for FDM 3D printing
  • Minimal backlash and good torque-to-size ratio

Next Steps

The next phase involves completing the assembly and evaluating the printed prototype under load conditions. Improvements will focus on structural strength, compactness, and stackability to build gearboxes for robotic joint actuation. Integration with sensors and drivers is planned for future iterations.

Tools Used

  • SolidWorks – CAD Modeling and Assembly
  • Fusion 360 – Motion Simulation
  • Creality Ender 3 – FDM 3D Printing
