Pixie: 4-DOF Robotic Manipulator

A lightweight robotic arm design with modularity and embedded control in mind.

Overview

Pixie is a 4-DOF robotic manipulator currently in development. The design emphasizes modularity, ease of assembly, and integration with embedded motor drivers and sensors. Intended as a low-cost platform for prototyping robotic behaviors, Pixie is expected to be equipped with a camera at the end effector for visual tracking and learning-based tasks.

Design Features

  • Uses NEMA 17 and Nema 23 stepper motors coupled with custom-designed cycloidal gearboxes for increased torque
  • Compact design for desktop-scale manipulation tasks
  • Utilizes TMC 2130 for enhanced stepper current control, and motor load feedback for human-friendly application
  • Currently in early-stage, big updates expected after July 25th weekend!
  • Ultimate aim is to develop arm for object tracking and imitation learning applications

Future Applications

The long-term goal for Pixie is to integrate camera-based object tracking and apply imitation learning techniques for pick-and-place or interaction-based control. The manipulator will act as a testbed for vision-guided robotic learning, with an emphasis on adaptability and modular control architecture.
